Product Variants

Foundation Bolt Types We Export

IS 5624 defines five standard foundation bolt types for different anchoring configurations and load requirements.

Type A — Bent Bolt

Hook or bent-end bolt cast in concrete. Simple, economical, and most commonly specified per IS 5624.

Type B — Plate Bolt

Bolt with a welded steel anchor plate at the base. Provides higher pull-out resistance than bent types.

Type D — Cotter & Sleeve

Sleeve-and-cotter assembly allowing positional adjustment after concrete pour. Used in precise machinery alignment.

Type E — Forged Head

Forged collar or head at the embedded end. Highest pull-out strength for seismic and dynamic load conditions.

Double-End Stud

Threaded both ends for leveling nuts above and below the base plate. Used in precision machinery installation.

Custom / Drawing-Based

Non-standard head shapes, lengths, and bends manufactured to engineer-supplied drawings for EPC projects.

Material Grades & Options

Carbon Steel

ASTM F1554 Gr. 36 & 55. Standard for most civil and industrial foundations. HDG coating for buried or outdoor exposure.

Stainless Steel 304

For foundations in moist, coastal, or mildly corrosive environments. Avoids long-term galvanic corrosion in sensitive structures.

Stainless Steel 316

Marine, waterfront, and chemical exposure. Superior chloride resistance for seawater-proximate structures and process plants.
